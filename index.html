Learning of The Risks


In the United States, e-waste represents only 2-3% of America's annual solid waste. However, this small percentage is also responsible for 70% of the nation's hazardous materials found in landfills, according to the EPA. Globally, electronic waste generation has been steadily increasing, reaching approximately 54 million metric tons in 2024. In 2024, globally, only 20% of all e-waste was documented, collected and eventually recycled. E-waste, when processed incorrectly, can produce harmful chemicals such as Mercury, Arsenic, Lead, and Chromium - leading to dangerous living environments and long-term health issues.
